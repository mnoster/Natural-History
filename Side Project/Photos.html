Photos

  • Daphnia

    The commonly refferred to "water flea" is found in many freshwater bodies. The transparency of the daphnia's skin allows us to see the functioning organs inside. Often the daphnia is carrying multiple eggs which can be seen in this photo on the middle right region of its body.
  • Copepod

    The copepod often accompanies the daphnia in the freshwater environment. It is a common source of nutrients among the fish. It falls under the classification of a crustacean and can be found in most bodies of water. Their diet consists mainly of phytoplankton.
  • Mosquito Larvae

    Ususally found in still fresh bodies of water are mosquito larvae. When removed from water they sqirm around choatically like a fish out of water. They can survive in the worst and best of water conditions, hence their presence all throughout the world
