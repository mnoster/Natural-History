Micro/Macro-invertebrates

Microinvertebrates

(ABOVE) The Copepod, Daphnia(water flea), Hyrda, and Rotifer are classified as freshwater Zooplankton. They also fall into the category of Microinvertebrates beacuse they do not have a spine. Zooplankton are one of the primary food sources for fish and are usually a sign of a healthy environment.

Macroinvertebrates

Mosquito Larve, Midgefly Larve, and Dragonfly Larve are some of the common types of larve found in freshwater. When they begin to mature and develop wings, they shed the outermost layer of skin and leave it behind as seen in with the midgefly in the last photo.

**The only difference between the Macro and Microinvertebrates is that the Macroinvertebrates are easily visible to the naked eye, where as the Microinvertebrates are much harder to see.
